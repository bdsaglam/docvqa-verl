**Goal:** SFT Qwen3.5-4B on 27B-teacher CodeAct trajectories (rejection-sampled,
strict ANLS==1.0, all 426 trajectories kept), sweep LoRA rank × LR, pick the best
config, full-val it vs the base-4B baseline.

## Setup

- **Data:** `data/sft/teacher_pool.parquet` — 426 teacher trajectories (anls==1.0),
  page mix incl. real multi-page docs; token lens median ~2.3K, max ~23K.
- **Training:** 4-GPU FSDP, flash-attn + remove-padding + dynamic-bsz@24576, GDN
  kernels (fla + causal-conv1d — the ~12-18× throughput fix), LoRA all-linear,
  8 epochs, constant LR, ckpt every 2 epochs (ep2/4/6/8 = global_step 52/104/156/208),
  effective batch = 16 samples. ~7s/step, ~26 min/config.
- **Configs (5):** rank/LR sweep around the baseline recipe (rank32, LR2e-4).

## Config ranking (mini, 13 questions / 1-per-doc, n=2)

Ranking funnel: a 13-question subset (one per doc, full category coverage), n=2,
to pick the best config fast (everything is bottlenecked by the remote 27B VLM
perception throughput). **Noisy (n=2, 13 hard Qs) — used only to rank.**

| config | rank | LR | mini ANLS (rank13, n2) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **sft-r32-lr1e4** | 32 | **1e-4** | **0.231** ← winner |
| sft-r16 | 16 | 2e-4 | ~0.208 |
| sft-r64 | 64 | 2e-4 | 0.115 |
| sft-r32 | 32 | 2e-4 | 0.077 |
| sft-r32-lr4e4 | 32 | 4e-4 | (skipped — predicted worst by LR trend) |

**Finding: gentler SFT wins.** The lowest-LR config (LR1e-4) and the smallest-rank
config (rank16) lead; the more aggressive configs (LR2e-4 at rank32/64, lower train
loss) score near zero. Interpretation: aggressive trajectory-imitation overfits and
erodes the base model's general agentic capability; gentle adaptation preserves it.
Consistent with the earlier SFT investigation (SFT-on-trajectories does not clearly
beat untrained base-4B; multi-turn observation-shift defeats imitation).

## Full-val (headline) — WINNER vs baseline

Winner **sft-r32-lr1e4 ep8** on the full 80-question val set, n=4.

| model | full-val ANLS (n) | pass@k | SC@k | cap rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| base-4B baseline | 0.2234 ± 0.0344 (n=8) | pass@8 0.550 | SC@8 0.263 | unknown (data deleted) |
| sft-r32-lr1e4 (rank13 winner) | 0.151 (n=1, 73/80Q) | — | — | ~47% capped |
| sft-r32-lr4e4 (lr4e4) ep8 — raw | 0.166 (n=4, 80Q) | pass@4 0.325 | SC@4 0.238 | **25.6%** |
| **sft-r32-lr4e4 (lr4e4) ep8 — cap-relieved** | **0.191 (n=4, 80Q)** | pass@4 0.350 | SC@4 0.238 | **6.9%** |

**lr4e4 full-val n=4: raw 16.6% → cap-relieved 19.1%.** The raw 16.6% was a *measurement
artifact*: 25.6% of rollouts hit the 1200s `wall_cap` timeout and scored 0 (censored, not
wrong). Re-running ONLY the timed-out slots at timeout=2400s + low concurrency
(`eval.py --rerun-terminations wall_cap,token_cap`, sample-level resume) recovered 61 of 83,
dropping the cap rate to 6.9% and lifting mean ANLS to **19.1%**. The remaining 6.9% (22
slots) re-capped even at 2400s — genuinely pathological heavy docs where the scaffold fans
out unbounded `batch_look` calls (token_cap hit 374 VLM calls) and never terminates; more
wall-clock won't fix these (they need a perception-*call* budget). Submit-only ANLS held
~23–25% throughout (raw 24.8% → relieved 23.2%) — that's the stable capability signal.

**Verdict: SFT ≈ base.** Cap-relieved lr4e4 (19.1% overall, ~23% submit-only) sits at/just
below base-4B (22.34%), and **the gap is not trustworthy** — base ran n=8 with an *unknown*
(un-relievable, data deleted) cap rate, so it carries the same artifact. SFT does **not**
clearly beat the untrained base; the apparent "−5.8pp" was mostly timeouts.

**Methodology note (see CLAUDE.md "Evaluation methodology"):** lr4e4 *topped* the rank13/n2
ranking (26.9%) — the mini was directionally representative of the ~true ~20–25% capability,
**not** "noise." The apparent mini→full *degradation* (27→16.6%) was a cap artifact, not a
real drop; once relieved, mini and full agree. Lesson: a mini→full drop in a project with
known timeout issues means investigate the measurement first, don't impeach the mini.

**Caveat — not directly comparable.** The SFT winner full-val ran at **n=1** (VLM
perception throughput is the hard ceiling; n=4/n=8 was ~4–10h). At n=1, **~47% of
rollouts hit wall/iter/token caps** (single deep multi-turn rollout times out on hard
multi-page docs) and score 0 — whereas the **n=8 baseline recovers** most of those (≥1
of 8 tries usually submits). So 0.151 (n1) is a **lower bound** that understates the
model vs the n8 baseline mean. Matched-n comparison was infeasible in the time budget.

**Verdict: SFT ≈ baseline, and the configs are statistically indistinguishable at this
eval power.** rank13 at n=2 has SE ≈ ±10pp, and the full LR×rank×epoch ladder (below) is
scatter around the base-4B level (~22%) with **no LR/rank/epoch trend**. Consistent with
the entire prior SFT investigation (SFT-on-teacher-trajectories ties, does not beat,
untrained base-4B). The properly-powered comparison is the **n=8 full-val** (pending). On-
policy RL is the lever tested next.

### RETRACTION: the "catastrophic-forgetting / gentle-wins" narrative does NOT hold

An earlier draft of this card argued aggressive SFT catastrophically forgets and
mode-collapses while gentle SFT preserves diversity. **The full ladder refutes that — it
was a noise artifact of a 3-point slice.**
- The two *most aggressive* configs land at **opposite extremes**: lr2e4 = 7.7% (worst),
  lr4e4 = **26.9% (best, above baseline)** — same tiny train loss (~0.05). The
  lowest-train-loss config *topping* the means is the direct counterexample to "more fit =
  more forgetting."
- The "aggressive collapses (pass@2 = mean)" signature was **only lr2e4**; lr4e4 (equally
  aggressive) has full diversity (pass@2 30.8% > mean 26.9%). One noisy config, not a rule.
- The gentle configs' "diversity gap" (+3.8–7.7pp) was **1–2 questions out of 13 at n=2** —
  far too thin to support a claim.

**What actually survives:** SFT lands ≈ baseline; at rank13/n2 the configs are
**statistically indistinguishable** and no LR/rank/epoch trend is measurable. Any apparent
ranking (incl. "r32-lr1e4 leads") is within noise.

### Properly-powered follow-up
rank13/n2 is selection-grade and cannot rank configs. The report SFT number(s) come from

comparable to the base-4B n=8 baseline (22.34% ± 3.44).

## Notes / gotchas recorded

- SFT checkpoint layout is FLAT (`global_step_N/model_world_size_*.pt`, no `actor/`
  subdir) — merge via `verl.model_merger merge --backend fsdp --local_dir global_step_N`.
- eval.py `--model` is both the API id AND the tokenizer source — pass a real path.
- results.json schema = `{summary:{overall_accuracy,...}, config, documents}`.
- Eval/RL are VLM-perception-bound: ~24 concurrent rollouts saturate the remote 27B;
  more just starve each rollout (→ wall_cap). Parallel agent lanes don't add throughput.
