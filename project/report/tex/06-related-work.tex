\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Reinforcement learning for language models.} Group-relative policy optimization (GRPO) replaces PPO's learned critic with a group-of-samples baseline, estimating advantages from the reward spread within a sampled group \citep{grpo}. A line of subsequent work refines its surrogate and normalization. Dr.GRPO removes length and standard-deviation normalizers that bias the gradient toward longer or lower-variance responses \citep{drgrpo}; DAPO contributes decoupled clipping, dynamic sampling, and token-level loss for stable long-CoT training \citep{dapo}; GSPO moves importance weighting from the token to the sequence level \citep{gspo}; and CISPO clips importance-sampling weights rather than token updates to retain all-token signal \citep{cispo}. The reinforcement-learning stage left to future work (\S\ref{sec:training}) would adopt GRPO with an ANLS verifier reward, and these variants delimit the design space for its surrogate.

\textbf{Knowledge distillation.} Sequence-level distillation (SeqKD) trains a student on teacher-generated outputs, an off-policy objective dense in tokens but disconnected from the student's own distribution. MiniLLM minimizes a reverse KL to avoid the mode-covering failure of forward KL on generative tasks \citep{minillm}, and generalized knowledge distillation (GKD) trains on student-sampled sequences to close the train--inference distribution gap \citep{gkd}. On-policy distillation extends this lineage to a dense per-token teacher signal computed over student rollouts \citep{opd}. This SeqKD~/ on-policy-distillation contrast frames the signals explored in \S\ref{sec:training}.

\textbf{Reinforcement learning under agent-controlled context.} Policy-gradient training assumes a single growing token sequence; harnesses in which the agent rewrites or compresses its own history break that assumption, and an emerging, not-yet-established line of work targets this regime directly. FoldAct shows that summary actions induce a policy-dependent, non-stationary observation distribution and repairs the gradient with purpose-built machinery \citep{foldact}; ReSum segments the trajectory at each compression and broadcasts trajectory-level advantage across segments \citep{resum}; ContextCurator reports that an untrained context manager underperforms full-context prompting and overtakes it only after reinforcement learning \citep{contextcurator}; and AdaCoM adapts the compression policy during agentic reasoning \citep{adacom}. Format-sensitivity of frozen models under prompt rewriting motivates the same concern \citep{sclar}. \S\ref{sec:scaffold} positions the append-only CodeAct scaffold against this frontier, choosing the prefix-preserving path for which mature training applies and leaving context-managing harnesses to future work.

\textbf{Agentic document question answering.} Recent systems pair a frozen vision--language model with an orchestrating agent. RVLM \citep{rvlm} drives perception through repeated on-demand calls to a frozen VLM perception tool --- the same perception primitive used by the RLM harness we reuse \citep{rlm}, a distinct system despite the one-letter-apart name. Multi-agent and tool-routing designs decompose document understanding across specialized roles \citep{madqa,mdocagent,orca,slideagent}, while others add retrieval, visual search, or learned OCR control \citep{arial,visor,docvstar,agenticocr}. The scaffold here builds on the CodeAct paradigm of expressing agent actions as executable code and on the ReAct interleaving of reasoning and action \citep{react}.

\textbf{DocVQA-family benchmarks.} The training-data pool of \S\ref{sec:training} and the evaluation of \S\ref{sec:background} draw on the document-VQA benchmark lineage: single-page DocVQA \citep{docvqa}, infographic and chart variants \citep{infographicvqa,chartqa}, multi-page and multi-document settings \citep{mpdocvqa,dude}, slide decks \citep{slidevqa}, and the long-document MMLongBench-Doc used for transfer trajectories \citep{mmlongbench}. Scoring follows the ANLS metric introduced with ST-VQA \citep{stvqa}.
